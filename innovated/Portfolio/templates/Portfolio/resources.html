Frequently Asked Compliance Questions

Quick reference answers for statutory threshold applicability and rules

What is the applicability threshold for the Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) Act, 1952?
The EPF Act applies to any establishment that employs 20 or more people. In such cases, both the employer and employee share standard contributions (typically 12% of the basic salary + dearness allowance). For establishments with fewer than 20 employees, voluntary registration can be pursued.
Under the POSH Act 2013, is it mandatory to have an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)?
Yes, it is legally mandatory for any office, business, or commercial establishment employing 10 or more staff members to establish an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC). The committee must be headed by a senior woman employee and include an external independent member.
What is the contribution rate and wage ceiling for Employees' State Insurance (ESI)?
The statutory contribution rate stands at 4.0% of gross wages (3.25% contributed by the employer and 0.75% by the employee). This is applicable for all employees whose gross monthly salary/wages do not exceed Rs. 21,000.
When is Gratuity payable to an employee and how is it calculated?
Gratuity is payable to an employee upon separation (due to resignation, retirement, or superannuation) after they have completed a minimum of 5 years of continuous service. It is calculated based on 15 days of the last-drawn basic wage for every completed year of service, capped up to a maximum limit of Rs. 20 Lakhs.
